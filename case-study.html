The Catalyst: Breaking the Pop-up

The Insight: During a shadowing session with Customer Success Managers (CSMs), I noticed they were struggling. Configuring a single user involved jumping between tabs and manually reconciling data.

The Business Trigger: We signed a new major Enterprise client. They required a "Multi-Company" structure where one user could be an Admin in Company A but just a Viewer in Company B.

The Limit: Our existing configuration lived in a small modal (pop-up). With the new client bringing 300+ departments and locations, the pop-up physically couldn't hold the data.
System Alert
UI Overflow Error
Cannot display 342 departments in modal view.

The Solution: Multi-Step Wizard

To handle the complexity without overwhelming the admin, I broke the flow into 6 logic steps. We moved from a "Modal" to a "Full Page" experience.

1

Separating Global vs. Local Data

We realized that "Name" and "Job Title" are global (belong to the person), while "Roles" and "Departments" are local (belong to the company).

User: Anna
├── Global: Product Designer
├── Company A: Admin (Dept: Design)
└── Company B: Viewer (Dept: Marketing)
2

Solving the "Click Fatigue"

With 5+ companies, selecting locations for each one is tedious. I added an "Apply to All" logic.

Apply settings to all linked companies
3

Step-by-Step Validation

Instead of one giant "Save" button that might fail, each step (Roles, 2FA, Vacation Mode) validates individually. This prevents data conflicts before they happen.

Strategic Pragmatism

The Constraint: We needed to ship before the Enterprise client onboarded. We didn't have time to update the entire Design System.

The Decision: I chose to use our legacy UI components (buttons, inputs) inside the new logic flow. This allowed Engineering to build 3x faster, ensuring we met the deadline.

Outcomes

  • 01

    CSM Efficiency Doubled Customer Success Managers can now handle 2x the client volume because they aren't stuck doing manual data entry.

  • 02

    Enterprise Acquisition The new architecture successfully onboarded the target client with 300+ departments without a single UI break.
